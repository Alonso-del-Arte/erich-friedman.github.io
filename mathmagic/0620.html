Problem of the Month (June 2020)

What is the smallest square that n squares of side 2 and m squares of side 1 can be packed into? The answer is often the smallest integer k with 4n+m ≤ k2 and n ≤ k/22, but we are interested in the other cases. What about packing these in circles instead?
