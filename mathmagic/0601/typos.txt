the digits of m and p in order are printer error numbersif the following condition is met (where [ab] is defined as the numbercomposed of the digits of a and b consecutively and in order):-  n=[XmpY], where X and Y each stand for zero or more multi-digit numbers             that begin with a 1 (but not with 10) or end with a 0.this is easily verified, as any number may be multiplied by 1,and thus by 1^x or y^0 for any x and y.(this is true for any base, and can be extended ton=m^p*q=[XmpYq], n=m^p*q^r=[XmpYqrZ], etc.)thus:   p             p  0 p               p  a p               p  p 0n=m =[mp], or n=m =a m =[a0mp], or n=m =1 m =[1amp], or n=m =m a =[mpa0],      p  0 b p 0 dor n=m =a 1 m c 1 =[a01bmpc01d], etc.the binary examples shown above are of the following formats:m^p*q^r=[mpXqr] (m^p*q=[mpXq]), m^p=[Xmp], and m^p=[mpX] (m^p*q=[mpXq]).numbers marked with "#" can also be written with repeated exponentiation,as 1^p^q=1^[pq].(i admit that printer
