Here's an easy way to memorize them. Separate them into vowel-consonant, consonant-vowel, vowel-vowel, and consonant-consonant categories:

vowel-consonant:
A : BDGHLMNRSTWXY
E : DFHLMNRSTWX
I : DFNST
O : DFHKMNPRSWXY
U : HMNPST
consonant-vowel:
 BDFHLKMNPTYZ : A
  BDFHMNPRTWY : E
  BGHKLMPQSTX : I
BDGHJLMNPSTWY : O
          MNX : U
vowel-vowel:
AA  AE  AI  OE  OI
consonant-consonant:
SH  MY  MM  HM  BY

There are only 5 each in the latter two categories, so just memorize them. For vowel-vowel, they start with A and O, and end with E and I, together with the first 2-letter word in the dictionary AA. For consonant-consonant, BY and MY are common words and SH, HM, MM are all sounds where each word starts with the letter the previous word ends with.
